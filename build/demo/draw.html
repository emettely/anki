What’s special about the way we’re layering in Racket Lang’s images teachpack?

2htdp/image

(require 2htdp/image)

Lisp feels a bit back-to-front and inside-out when you first start! Our background is the innermost item, and our rocket the outermost (wrapped in place-image function)

(require 2htdp/image)

; Here's the constants

(define WIDTH 180)
(define HEIGHT 180)
(define MIDDLE (/ WIDTH 2))

(define BACKGROUND
  (rectangle WIDTH HEIGHT "solid" "black"))

(define ROCKET
  (bitmap/file "rocket.png"))

(define MOON
  (circle 40 "solid" "white"))

; Let's make our image!

(place-image
  MOON 10 10
    (place-image
      ROCKET MIDDLE MIDDLE
        BACKGROUND))
__akf-3.0.0::lisp

Lisp calculates the inner-most function (data or variable) FIRST. It then works inside-out to render our ROCKET.
